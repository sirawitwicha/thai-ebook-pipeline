# Worked Example: Data → Opening Paragraph Transformations

**IMPORTANT — This skill is NICHE-AGNOSTIC**: These examples use "dark psychology of office politics" data to demonstrate the PATTERN. Your niche will have different slang, different pain, different enemies. The extraction process is identical regardless of niche.

---

## Example 1: "Let me tell you a story"

**Source**: `copy-brief.md` → "The Bar Complaint Monologue" (first paragraph)

**Raw data**:
> "I didn't sign up for this. I went to school, I got good grades, I got a good job, and I thought that was the deal — you work hard, you get ahead. Nobody told me there was a WHOLE OTHER GAME happening right in front of me that I wasn't even seeing."

**Why this is opener material**: This IS an opening paragraph already written by the market. It's a complete story setup: the innocent beginning, the dawning realization, the hidden game. All we do is frame it.

**Opener type**: Let me tell you a story

**Result**:
> Let me tell you a story about someone who did everything right. Went to school. Got good grades. Got the job. Believed — genuinely believed — that if you just work hard and do great work, you get ahead.
>
> And then watched, year after year, as people who did half the work got twice the recognition.
>
> Nobody told her there was a whole other game happening right in front of her. A game with rules she was never taught.

## The Adaptation Rule

Every example above uses the "dark psychology of office politics" research data. When you work on a DIFFERENT niche, the data changes but the method doesn't:

- For keto diet: the enemy might be "the diet industry." The bar complaint is "I've tried every diet and nothing works." The proof is Mayo Clinic, not Machiavelli.
- For golf swing: the enemy is "the golf instructor who overcomplicates everything." The bar complaint is "I've spent thousands on lessons and I'm worse than when I started."
- For dog training: the enemy is "the pet store trainer who uses outdated dominance theory." The "Do You Know?" is "Do you know you should never let your dog walk on your driveway?"

**Same formulas. Different raw material. That's the point.**
