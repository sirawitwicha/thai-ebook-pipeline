# Ben Settle's 45 Market Research Questions

From *Copy Slacker* by Ben Settle — "Criminally Profitable Research" methodology. These are the questions to answer about any market before writing copy. The goal: create a "composite customer" — the ideal buyer you're selling to.

**Core principle**: Observe what they BUY and DO, not what they SAY. Surveys lie. Purchase behavior tells the truth.

---

## Chunk 1: Pain & Fear Profile

### Q1: What keeps them awake at night?
Sunday night insomnia. What do they stare at the ceiling about? What do they roll over and tell their spouse? This alone gives you so much copy fodder it becomes about what to leave OUT.

### Q2: What are they terrified of? (in order)
Their "house of horrors." What they admit to + what they're too ashamed to admit. Most fears are irrational — and people pay irrational amounts to eliminate them.

### Q3: What causes them pain?
Physical, emotional, psychological, spiritual pain. What's causing pain right now? How does your product help?

### Q4: What are their insecurities?
Everyone has insecurities they hope nobody discovers. Making a long list and working them into copy is one of the most profitable things you can do.

### Q5: What makes them angry?
Everyone gets angry about something. Find the anger triggers. Write them in a big list.

### Q6: Who exactly are they angry at?
A newscaster? Boss? Ex-spouse? Government? Gurus who screwed them? Uncovering this is vital for establishing a common enemy in your copy.

### Q7: Who are they blaming for their problems?
Most people refuse to take responsibility. Real or imagined, rational or irrational — know who they blame.

### Q8: What do they worry about most?
Not just fears. The nagging, pressing, terrifying worries. Children kidnapped? Economy collapsing? Supplement discontinued? Everyone worries about something.

### Q11: What's their most urgent hot button crisis right now?
People live crisis-to-crisis. In financial markets, this changes minute to minute. What's the crisis NOW, and how does your product affect it?

### Q15: What three things frustrate them the most each day?
From losing car keys to hellish commutes to a farting dog. Find the top 3 frustrations. Doing so helps prevent YOU being frustrated by low sales.

### Q24: What do they feel guilty about?
Not spending time with kids? Watching porn instead of working? Spending on frivolous things? Being born with money? Even if it doesn't make it into copy, knowing this shapes everything.

## Chunk 2: Identity & Worldview

### Q9: What are their values like?
Never project YOUR values onto them. Find THEIR values, understand them (even if you disagree), and make sure your copy doesn't violate them. Nothing gets copy deleted faster.

### Q10: What religion are they?
Christian? Muslim? Atheist? This affects how you approach copy. Don't mock or marginalize their beliefs.

### Q13: What income bracket are they in?
Uphill battle selling high-ticket to broke people. High-income people think differently about prestige/status vs necessity/bargains.

### Q14: What are their political views?
Republican? Democrat? Apolitical? Shapes values, buying decisions, and who they'll buy from or refuse to buy from.

### Q17: Who exactly are the main buyers for this product?
Go for the HOT people — the primary buyers. Don't water down copy trying to sell to everyone. Write TO the primary buyers.

### Q18: Who wears the pants in buying decisions?
Wife often makes the decisions. If your prospect needs "permission" to buy, you may be selling to multiple people, not one.

### Q19: How old is your customer?
Millennials, Gen X, Boomers? Talk differently to each generation.

### Q20: What gender is your customer?
Go for the primary buyers. Don't try to sell to men and women in the same ad if one is clearly the buyer.

### Q21: What is their occupation?
Blue collar, white collar, executive, student, unemployed? Dramatic effect on what you say and which appeals you use.

### Q22: What's their education level?
High school dropout? PhD? Gene Schwartz advised keeping words at 3 syllables or less for mass market. Jim Rutz ignored that for financial markets. Know your market.

### Q23: Are they in debt?
Maxed credit cards? Pay cash? Despise debt? Shapes how you create and present your offer.

### Q25: Where are they located?
What part of country/world? Gated community or low-rent apartment? Location matters even selling online.

### Q26: Do they own a home or rent?
Are they landlords or tenants? What's their living situation like?

### Q32: What slang do they use?
The more you sound like one of them, the more likely they buy. Know their slang, figures of speech, unique jargon. Sound like an outsider = nobody buys from an outsider.

### Q41: What roles do they play in life?
Boss, parent, lover, spouse? Leader, coach, adviser, teammate? Banker (pays for deadbeat), cleaning lady (cleans up after boyfriend), chauffeur?

## Chunk 3: Desires & Aspirations

### Q12: Who are their enemies?
Everyone has enemies. The office politician, a bureaucrat, a lazy mailman, an ex-spouse, a social media troll, a mother-in-law. Know their enemies, know more sales.

### Q16: What do they want more than anything?
Peace of mind? Eliminate money problems? Tell boss to stick it? Revenge on ex-lover? Security? Not what they SAY they want — what would they crawl through broken glass to have?

### Q31: What do they secretly fantasize about?
Strong body that makes women swoon? Giant house and boat? Fame, fortune, power? Everybody fantasizes about something. Work it into your copy.

### Q37: Do they crave recognition from anyone?
Who do they want to be recognized by? A parent? A boss? A former doubter? Dig this out.

### Q38: Who do they want to impress the most?
Whose "radar" do they want to be on? Woman? Boss? Friends? Spouse? Kids? Coach?

### Q39: What do they do for fun?
Ballgame or TV? Hiking or biking? Fishing, hunting, shooting? Opera or movies? Find what they ACTUALLY do, not what they say they do. Observe, don't ask.

---

## Chunk 4: Media & Culture Consumption

### Q34: What books are they reading?
Huge bearing on how you write ads — appeals they respond to, what entertains them, topics to reference.

### Q35: What movies are they watching?
Romance? Superhero? Horror? Foreign films with subtitles? May have zero direct bearing, but the more you know, the better.

### Q36: What TV shows are they watching?
Ben Settle used Claritas Report data showing his self-defense market watched "24" — so he referenced Jack Bauer in the ad. That kind of detail makes copy resonate.

### Q44: Do they hang out in forums or chat rooms?
"The single most powerful research tool I've ever used." Go lurk. People say things in forums they'd never tell their spouse. They hand you stories, words, problems, and entire headlines in their own vernacular.

---

## Chunk 5: Competitive & Market Intelligence

### Q28: What's the #1 most urgent problem they're trying to solve?
Narrow to one overarching, urgent problem. If you can do this, 80% of your job is done before writing a word. Shapes everything.

### Q33: Who else is successfully selling something similar, and how?
Read their ads. What's working? What's not? Where are the holes due to their lack of research? How can you do it better or completely differently?

### Q42: Are they looking to be sold, or looking for information?
Changes your entire approach — report-style vs pitch-style. Affects headline, structure, everything.

### Q43: What other related products are they buying?
Affects sales copy AND gives you upsell ideas. The direct mail industry has done this for decades — rent customer lists. If someone just bought a book on confidence, they'll likely buy yours too.

### Q45: How aware are they of the problem? (scale 1-10)
Gene Schwartz's market awareness scale. Don't know they have a problem → know they have it → looking for solution → want YOUR product specifically. Each stage requires completely different copy.

---

## Chunk 6: Emotional & Decision Drivers

### Q29: What feelings are they experiencing during search?
What emotions when they type a keyword and your site pops up? When they click a link? When they open your envelope? Huge impact on your headline.

### Q30: How do you want them to feel when they see your sales letter?
All decisions are emotional. The decision to be "logical" is itself emotional. Build your pitch around the desired feeling.

### Q40: What do they complain about to friends over drinks?
Picture them at the bar explaining the problem your product solves. What exact words? What emotions? What look on their face? This is your headline in raw form.

---

## How to know you've done enough research

Ben Settle's test: **When you can write most of your sales copy without even seeing the product.**

Old school copywriters wrote ads completely "blind" — they'd write the ad first, promising everything the market wanted, THEN build the product to fulfill those promises. This only works if your market research is thorough.

*"It's not about the weedkiller. It's about the crab grass."*
