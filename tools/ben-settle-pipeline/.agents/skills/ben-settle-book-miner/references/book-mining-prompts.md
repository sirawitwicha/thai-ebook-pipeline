# Book Mining Prompts — 6 Adapted Chunks + Final Assembly

Same 45 questions as `ben-settle-research/references/synthesis-prompt.md`, but each chunk is fed BOOK PAGES as data instead of internet scrapes. The output format is identical so the downstream pipeline runs unchanged.

**Key difference from internet research**: Internet data is raw, emotional, and scattered. Book data is structured, systematic, and deep. The prompts below are tuned to extract the AUTHOR'S frameworks, named techniques, and unique metaphors — not just raw complaints.

---

## Chunk 1: Pain & Fear Profile
**Questions covered**: Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4, Q5, Q6, Q7, Q8, Q11, Q15, Q24
**Feed these pages**: Introduction, early chapters where the author diagnoses the reader's problem, case studies or "before" descriptions, any section titled "why most people fail" or "the problem with..."

```
You are a direct response copywriter doing Ben Settle-style market research — but instead of scraping internet comments, you are mining a BOOK written by a domain expert. The book pages below contain the author's systematized diagnosis of the reader's pain, fears, and frustrations.

Read the book pages carefully. Answer each question using ONLY what the author describes. If the author doesn't address a question, say "Not covered in these pages" — do not invent.

QUESTIONS TO ANSWER:

1. WHAT KEEPS THEM AWAKE AT NIGHT? — What does the author say the reader stares at the ceiling about? Quote the author's exact descriptions.
2. TOP TERRORS (in order) — What fears does the author name? Both rational and irrational. The author may explicitly list these.
3. WHAT CAUSES THEM PAIN? — Physical, emotional, psychological, spiritual. Extract from the author's descriptions. Separate each type.
4. THEIR INSECURITIES — What does the author say the reader is embarrassed about? What do they hope nobody finds out?
5. WHAT MAKES THEM ANGRY? — What anger triggers does the author describe? Quote the exact language.
6. WHO ARE THEY ANGRY AT? — Specific targets the author names: types of people, institutions, systems, mindsets.
7. WHO DO THEY BLAME? — Who or what does the author say the reader blames for their problems? Even if the author says this blame is misplaced.
8. WHAT DO THEY WORRY ABOUT MOST? — Nagging worries the author describes. Separate from Q1/Q2.
11. URGENT HOT BUTTON CRISIS — What crisis does the author say the reader is facing RIGHT NOW?
15. TOP 3 DAILY FRUSTRATIONS — What daily frustrations does the author describe?
24. WHAT DO THEY FEEL GUILTY ABOUT? — What guilt or shame does the author name?

FORMAT YOUR ANSWER:
- For each question, list findings as bullet points
- Quote the author's exact phrases in "quotation marks" wherever possible
- Cite the page or section where you found each answer: "From Chapter 2, page X — 'exact quote'"
- Mark items as [CORE TECHNIQUE] if tied to a named method or framework the author teaches
- End with a "Pain Profile Summary" — the single most powerful pain/fear angle for this market, in the author's own framing

[BOOK PAGES — FEED RELEVANT PAGES HERE]
```

## Chunk 2: Identity & Worldview
**Questions covered**: Q9, Q10, Q13, Q14, Q17, Q18, Q19, Q20, Q21, Q22, Q23, Q25, Q26, Q32, Q41
**Feed these pages**: Introduction, "who this book is for" sections, cultural references throughout the book, the author's assumptions about the reader

```
You are a direct response copywriter mining a book to build a buyer persona. The author has spent years understanding who their reader is. The book pages below reveal the author's assumptions about the reader's identity, values, and worldview.

Read the book pages carefully. Answer each question using ONLY what the author reveals or implies about their reader.

QUESTIONS TO ANSWER:

9. WHAT ARE THEIR VALUES? — What does the author say the reader holds sacred? What lines won't they cross?
10. WHAT RELIGION ARE THEY? — Or secular/atheist/agnostic? How does the author's tone accommodate this?
13. INCOME BRACKET — What does the author assume about the reader's financial situation?
14. POLITICAL VIEWS — Any political leanings implied or stated?
17. WHO ARE THE MAIN BUYERS? — Describe the PRIMARY reader the author is writing for. The HOT buyer.
18. WHO MAKES BUYING DECISIONS? — Does the author assume the reader decides alone or with others?
19. HOW OLD ARE THEY? — What generation/age range does the author target?
20. GENDER — Who is the primary reader? Is the language gendered or neutral?
21. OCCUPATION — What profession/role does the author assume?
22. EDUCATION LEVEL — What reading level does the author write at? Sophisticated or accessible?
23. ARE THEY IN DEBT? — Any assumptions about the reader's financial health?
25. WHERE ARE THEY LOCATED? — Geography, urban/suburban/rural, country?
26. HOME OWNERSHIP — Any assumptions?
32. SLANG & VERNACULAR — Extract EVERY unique term, phrase, metaphor, coined word, technique name, and figure of speech the author uses. This is the highest-value ammunition in this chunk. Build a mini dictionary of 50+ entries. Quote exact phrases.
41. WHAT ROLES DO THEY PLAY? — What hats does the author assume the reader wears? Boss, parent, victim, striver, survivor?

FORMAT:
- Bullet points per question
- For Q32 (Vernacular): List EVERY term and phrase as a dictionary entry. This is CRITICAL — the author's language IS your copy language.
- Cite page sources: "From page X"
- End with a "Reader Persona Summary" — a 3-sentence composite of who this book was written for

[BOOK PAGES — FEED RELEVANT PAGES HERE]
```

## Chunk 3: Desires & Aspirations
**Questions covered**: Q12, Q16, Q31, Q37, Q38, Q39
**Feed these pages**: Later chapters where the author describes the transformation, "what you'll achieve," chapter conclusions, the book's promise, testimonials or case studies within the book

```
You are a direct response copywriter uncovering what a market secretly desires — from a domain expert's book. The author has systematized the reader's desires into frameworks and promises. The book pages below contain the author's version of "the promised land."

Read the book pages carefully. Extract both stated and implied desires.

QUESTIONS TO ANSWER:

12. WHO ARE THEIR ENEMIES? — Every enemy the author names. Types of people, institutions, mindsets, "the people who..." — these are targets to rail against in copy. Be specific.
16. WHAT DO THEY WANT MORE THAN ANYTHING? — The author's core promise. What's the ultimate outcome the book delivers?
31. WHAT DO THEY SECRETLY FANTASIZE ABOUT? — What does the author hint is possible that the reader might be too embarrassed to admit wanting? Power? Control? Recognition? Revenge? Freedom?
37. WHO DO THEY CRAVE RECOGNITION FROM? — Whose approval does the author say matters most?
38. WHO DO THEY WANT TO IMPRESS? — Who is the reader performing for, according to the author?
39. WHAT DO THEY DO FOR FUN? — Any lifestyle details the author reveals or assumes?

FORMAT:
- For each question, separate what the author STATES from what the author IMPLIES
- Quote aspirational language directly: "the author promises the reader will be able to..." 
- Cite page sources
- End with a "Core Desire Statement" — one sentence capturing the book's ultimate promise, in the author's language

[BOOK PAGES — FEED RELEVANT PAGES HERE]
```

## Chunk 4: Media & Culture
**Questions covered**: Q34, Q35, Q36, Q44
**Feed these pages**: THE ENTIRE BOOK. Every page is a source for cultural references. Scan for: mentions of books, authors, movies, TV shows, historical figures, philosophers, celebrities, communities, platforms.

```
You are a direct response copywriter mapping a book's cultural references. Every book, movie, historical figure, or community the author mentions is ammunition for your copy — it's a reference that makes your sales letter feel native to the reader's world.

Read the book pages below. Extract EVERY cultural reference you find.

QUESTIONS TO ANSWER:

34. WHAT BOOKS DOES THE AUTHOR REFERENCE? — Specific titles, authors, philosophical works. These are credibility anchors you can reference in copy.
35. WHAT MOVIES ARE REFERENCED? — Specific films, characters, genres.
36. WHAT TV SHOWS ARE REFERENCED? — Specific shows, characters, cultural moments.
44. WHAT COMMUNITIES DOES THE AUTHOR MENTION? — Forums, platforms, physical spaces, professional groups, "where people like us gather."

FORMAT:
- List every reference found with the page it appears on
- Note whether the reference is used as: [AUTHORITY] (to build credibility), [CONTRAST] (as a negative example), [ANALOGY] (to explain a concept), or [CULTURAL TOUCHPOINT] (shared reference)
- End with a "Cultural Playbook" — the 5-10 references you could most naturally weave into copy for this market

[BOOK PAGES — FEED THE ENTIRE BOOK OR A BROAD SCAN]
```

## Chunk 5: Competitive & Market Intelligence
**Questions covered**: Q28, Q33, Q42, Q43, Q45
**Feed these pages**: Early chapters where the author discusses what DOESN'T work, competing approaches, failed solutions, "most people think X but actually Y," sections comparing the author's method to alternatives

```
You are a direct response copywriter analyzing the competitive landscape THROUGH the author's lens. The author has likely positioned their book against competing approaches — naming what doesn't work, why common advice fails, and why their method succeeds.

Read the book pages below. Extract competitive intelligence from the author's own positioning.

QUESTIONS TO ANSWER:

28. #1 URGENT PROBLEM THEY'RE TRYING TO SOLVE — Narrow it to ONE overarching problem the author says the reader is desperate to solve. Quote the author's framing.
33. WHAT COMPETING APPROACHES DOES THE AUTHOR MENTION? — What methods, books, gurus, or schools of thought does the author say DON'T work? What are their weaknesses according to the author? Where are the gaps the author claims to fill?
42. ARE THEY LOOKING TO BE SOLD OR LOOKING FOR INFO? — Based on the book's depth and tone, where on the awareness spectrum is the reader? Report-style (education-heavy) or pitch-style (ready to act)? What does the author assume the reader already knows?
43. WHAT RELATED PRODUCTS WOULD THE READER BUY? — What adjacent books, courses, tools, or services does the author's framing suggest the reader might already own or be shopping for?
45. MARKET AWARENESS (1-10 SCALE) — Gene Schwartz's scale:
    - 1-3: Don't know they have a problem
    - 4-6: Know they have a problem, researching solutions
    - 7-8: Know solutions exist, comparing options
    - 9-10: Know about specific products, ready to buy

FORMAT:
- For each competing approach: Name, What the author says is wrong with it, What the author claims instead
- End with a "Competitive Gap Analysis" — what is the unique claim only THIS book makes?

[BOOK PAGES — FEED RELEVANT PAGES HERE]
```

## Chunk 6: Emotional & Decision Drivers
**Questions covered**: Q29, Q30, Q40
**Feed these pages**: THE ENTIRE BOOK — but especially the introduction, conclusion, "imagine this" passages, and the most emotionally charged sections where the author is directly addressing the reader.

```
You are a direct response copywriter mapping the emotional journey of a buyer — from a domain expert's book. The author has designed the book to create a specific emotional transformation in the reader. Your job is to extract that emotional architecture.

Read the book pages below. Map the emotional journey.

QUESTIONS TO ANSWER:

29. WHAT FEELINGS IS THE READER EXPERIENCING WHEN THEY FIND THIS BOOK? — When they pick it up, when they read the title, when they flip through the first pages. What's their emotional state? Frustrated? Desperate? Curious? Skeptical? Defeated? Quote the author's own diagnosis of the reader's starting emotional state.

30. HOW DOES THE AUTHOR WANT THE READER TO FEEL AFTER READING? — What emotional transformation does the book promise? From [current state] → to [desired state]. Quote the author's own words about what the reader will become.

40. WHAT WOULD THE READER SAY TO A FRIEND OVER DRINKS? — If the reader were at a bar explaining their problem to a best friend, what exact words would they use? Write this in FIRST PERSON, using the vernacular and framing from the book. This is your "Bar Complaint Monologue" — the empathy anchor you read before every writing session.

FORMAT:
- For Q29: Vivid, sensory description of the starting emotional state — not clinical labels
- For Q30: Full emotional arc: Current emotional state → Trigger point (what made them pick up the book) → Journey (what the reading experience feels like) → Arrival (the promised emotional state)
- For Q40: Write the monologue in first person, 2-3 paragraphs long. Use the author's framing and language. Make it feel like someone who READ this book and is telling their friend why they needed it.
- End with a "Headline Goldmine" — 5-10 raw headline starters harvested from the author's most powerful emotional statements

[BOOK PAGES — FEED THE MOST EMOTIONALLY CHARGED SECTIONS]
```

## Final Assembly Prompt

Run this AFTER all 6 chunks are complete. Feed ALL chunk outputs together.

```
You are a direct response copywriter assembling a complete market research profile and copywriting brief from the 6 analysis chunks below. These chunks were produced by mining a domain expert's book — every answer came from the author's pages.

Using ONLY the chunk outputs provided, produce TWO deliverables:

## DELIVERABLE 1: Structured JSON Profile
Follow the schema from ben-settle-research (references/output-schemas.md). Every field that has data must be filled. Fields without data should be marked null. Do not invent data.

The JSON structure must include:
- research_metadata (niche, date, source_type: "book_mining", pages_mined: N)
- composite_customer (pain_and_fear, identity_and_worldview, desires_and_aspirations, emotional_drivers, media_and_culture, competitive_intelligence)
- primary_buyer_description
- copywriting_brief_seeds

## DELIVERABLE 2: Copywriting Brief (Markdown)

### Headline Starters (10)
Raw headline ideas derived from the author's most powerful statements. Use the author's exact phrasing. The best ones will feel like they came from the book itself.

### Primary Hook
The single strongest emotional angle. One sentence. Directly from the book's thesis.

### Pain Point Bullets (10)
In the author's exact language. Each should make the prospect think "that's exactly how I feel." Quote or paraphrase from the book's diagnosis sections.

### The Enemy
Who or what the author says the reader is fighting against. The author named this enemy — use their exact framing.

### Desire Anchors (5)
What the book promises. Phrased as outcomes: "How to [desire] without [feared tradeoff]"

### Slang & Vernacular Dictionary
Every unique term, technique name, metaphor, coined phrase, and figure of speech the author uses. 100+ entries. This is the highest-value ammunition for bullets. Group by category:
- Named Techniques & Frameworks
- Metaphors & Analogies
- Power & Influence Terms
- Enemy Labels & Descriptors
- Identity Terms
- Direct Quotes (the author's most quotable lines)

### Proof Elements Inventory
Every credibility marker the author uses: historical figures, academic studies, personal experience, case studies, statistics, named authorities.

### Objection Inventory
Every objection the author addresses — explicitly or implicitly. For each, note how the book neutralizes it.

### Tone Calibration
- Reading level: [grade level]
- Vocabulary: [simple/moderate/sophisticated]
- Emotional register: [fear-driven/aspiration-driven/anger-driven/etc]
- Pace: [urgent/measured/calm]
- Relationship: [friend/authority/coach/insider/outsider]
- Voice signature: [what makes this author's voice UNIQUE — the one thing you'd recognize blind]

### Competitive Positioning Map
For each competing approach the author mentions: their weakness according to the author, and the gap the author's book fills.

### The "Bar Complaint" Monologue
A 2-3 paragraph first-person narrative — IN THE AUTHOR'S VOICE — as if someone who needs this book is talking to a friend. This is your empathy anchor. Read it before every writing session.

[CHUNK 2 OUTPUT — IDENTITY & WORLDVIEW]
[CHUNK 3 OUTPUT — DESIRES & ASPIRATIONS]
[CHUNK 4 OUTPUT — MEDIA & CULTURE]
[CHUNK 5 OUTPUT — COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE]
[CHUNK 6 OUTPUT — EMOTIONAL & DECISION DRIVERS]
```
