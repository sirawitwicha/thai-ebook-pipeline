**Sales Letter Lead #4:**
**Brutal And Blatant Honesty**

Being brutally honest — especially in an industry where nobody is — can inspire trust in you faster than almost anything else. This is where you come out an admit the drawback of what you're selling. A lot of copywriters foolishly try to hide and downplay the bad stuff. But it's far better to go the opposite way.

A perfect example of this is the infamous Ernest Shackleton ad. He was a famous explorer back in the early 1900s. He ran a little classified ad that said:

**Men wanted for hazardous journey, small wages, bitter cold, safe return doubtful, honor and recognition in case of success.**

Some marketers say this ad is a myth and never happened.

But, from what I understand, it was not only very real, but got him a tsunami of replies from people all over England. Everybody wanted to be a part of his expedition. Yet, there's only one benefit in it, the rest are negative statements that would turn people off. You can't get more honest than that, or more blatant. This is pure, brutal honesty, and people love it. It practically forces people to realize you're not trying to screw them over. And so few people are willing to do this in their ads that the small handful of us who do make out pretty well.

This goes beyond your opening paragraph, too. Admitting flaws creates interest and belief in every aspect of your copy.
