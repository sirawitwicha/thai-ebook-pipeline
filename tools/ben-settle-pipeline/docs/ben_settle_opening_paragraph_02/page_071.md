One reason I love using these opening paragraphs is because they are “attention span friendly.”

And, like it or not, most people have gold fish-like attention spans.

Incidentally, I believe copywriters who have short attention spans have a huge advantage over copywriters who don’t because if something keeps our interest, chances are it will keep a prospect’s interest, too. That’s why these opening paragraphs are so good for jolting people’s attention.

It could be as simple as:

**“Picture this.”**

Another of my favorites is from the great Matt Furey who had a long-running ad that started with:

**“I was a total skeptic.”**

Very small, very easy to read, and very powerful.

Again don’t necessarily use these word-for-word. They are templates to twist and make your own, based on your market, and your personality.

**Sales Letter Opener #9:**
**Would you take advice from...?**

This one is also related to the question opening paragraph, and you ask if your prospect would take advice from someone they would normally avoid like the zombie apocalypse plague. And the more outrageous and dramatic you can make this, the better.

Here’s an example written by the great John Carlton:

**“Would you take investment advice from a drug dealer? Sounds like a silly question doesn’t it? Well don’t make up your mind too quickly. Because I’ve got a true story to tell you that sounds like something right out of the Godfather.”**

The entire ad is a fascinating case study.
