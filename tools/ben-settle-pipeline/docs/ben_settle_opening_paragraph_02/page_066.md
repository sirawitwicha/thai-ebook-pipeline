While we're on the subject, consider this:

The great Ken McCarthy taught in his magnificent copywriting course,
(which I highly recommend if you ever have a chance — see KenMcCarthy.com)
about how most markets can be broken down like this:

50% of a market will never buy from direct response no matter what you
say or what you offer. They're simply not going to buy from direct response. On
the other hand, 5% of a market will buy everything — i.e. the hyper buyers.
There could be some good people on there, but there's also a lot of crud... who
refund the most, complain the most, put the most demands on you, won't
implement whatever it is you're selling, or take whatever it is you're selling (if
it's a consumable thing) and complain about it and move on to the next thing.
On the other hand, you have as many as 2-5 times more would-be buyers who
are skeptical, and who want what you have, but need to be sold. They're not
going to waste their money on a mood, emotion, or impulse. This is at least
twice as big as the hyper buyers (who will buy no matter what in many cases).
And they don't like hype and bull crap. They don't like empty promises. They
want things proven and not look like an idiot by making bad decisions.

Thus, why adding proof to the If/Then opener works so well.

Here's an example I wrote for a self defense ad:

**"If you'd like to know a secret way to make yourself near invincible**
**in a fight, then here's how ancient gladiators and samurai did it**
**centuries ago. And why you can do the same thing today."**
