**Sales Letter Opener #7:**
**Do you know?**

This is a variation on the question opener, and asking this question has
a built in, “must read” curiosity if it’s about something relevant to your
prospect’s life she wants to know more about.

I first saw it used by the great Gary Bencivenga in an ad for a product
about how to get job interviews. It asked questions like “do you know why you
should never eat a spinach salad when you’re being interviewed over lunch?”

Anyone looking for a job would be all over that.

So, I adapted it for the dog market:

**“Do you know you should never give your dog a bone?”**

This also ties into using contrast, too. Everybody gives their dog a bone,
but I’m saying (at the top of the ad) why they shouldn’t. Followed by similar
questions regarding why they shouldn’t let their dog chew on tennis balls or
even walk on their driveways. And then going into how these innocent mistakes
can seriously injure, hurt, or even kill their dog.

But, realize this:

When you ask a question, it has to be something they really don’t know
and throws them for a loop. It’s going to rack that shotgun.

- Who doesn’t give their dog a bone?
- Who doesn’t let their dog chew on a tennis ball?
- Who doesn’t let their dog walk on their driveway?

Many prospects who love their dogs have to keep reading. And if you use
this opening paragraph correctly, based on your market research, you can get
that same kind of intense interest in reading your sales copy, too.
