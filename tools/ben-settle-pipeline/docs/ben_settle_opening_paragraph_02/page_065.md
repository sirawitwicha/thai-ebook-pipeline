**Sales Letter Lead #1:**
**“Let me tell you a story”**

My “old faithful” opener — as it doesn’t get any less complicated than starting your sales copy off with, word for word, “let me tell you a story.”

I use this in sales letters and in emails. It’s super simple. It’s crystal clear. And it gets to the point and people like to hear stories. So if you do the headline right, then follow it with “let me tell you a story”, and then start telling a fascinating story (see the next chapter), and resist trying to get clever and be cool like a lot of amateur copywriters do… your copy has a great chance of being read and, ultimately, bought from.

**Sales Letter Lead #2:**
**If/then**

This was almost exclusively used by the great Gary Halbert. If you study his ads (and you should), you will see he used this over and over and over. And one of the many reasons it works so well is it has built-in belief due to this qualifying word “if.”

When you put the word “if” before any claim you make (headline, bullets, anywhere else) it qualifies it. It takes away the “bull shit factor” of something. It says “you must do x to get y benefit” not “here’s how to get y benefit no matter what!”, which people instinctively know is not the case.

Here’s an example:

**If you would like to quickly double your sales using nothing but free Facebook posts, then this letter will show you how.**

It is astonishing how this little two letter word can take something that would normally sound like hype and bull crap and give it credibility. It’s powerful, believable, credible, and it gets right to the point. And that’s why I like using it so much. It also can do the same in other parts of your copy, too.
