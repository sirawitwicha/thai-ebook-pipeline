**A Warning About Sales Copy**
**Opening Paragraphs**

There’s a book called “The Game” by Neil Strauss — about his
adventures in the underground pickup artist industry back in the ‘90s, when it
wasn’t as well-known as it is now.

His code name was Style.

And, he got so good at it, he started teaching other guys how to pick up
women. Eventually, he had a huge following of people who wanted to learn
from him. And what they would do is what a lot of copywriters do now when
they learn information like templates, start building swipe files, and begin to
latch on to their favorite teachers:

**They Mindlessly Copied**
**Everything He Did**
**To The Point Where**
**It Stopped Working For Everyone!**

So for example:

He noticed one day in LA on the strip, there were groups of his students
that looked just like him (they shaved their heads like him, and grew a goatee
like him), dressed like him (same clothes, sun glasses, etc), and used the exact
same pick up lines as him out of context, and without any unique personality
— like robots.

Imagine that for a moment.

All these guys, all looking the exact same, saying the exact same lines,
and dressing the exact same, going to the exact same group of women, and
trying to pick them up.

They were laughed at and mocked.

And, rightfully so.
