But, if you do a lifetime guarantee, be as dramatic as possible about it.
My favorite example of that is John Carlton who once put in an ad something
like:

**“Your grandkids**
**can return it!”**

That's high drama.

And, a really interesting way of doing it, too.

Another good way to do a guarantee is to offer a refund plus you'll pay
them X amount of money for their trouble. It takes some balls to do this, but
it's worth testing if your product lends itself to something like this. You simply
say something like:

**“I'll refund your money plus pay you $100 dollars cash out of my**
**own pocket just for wasting your time!”**

Or, to make it even more ballsy and dramatic:

**“I'll give you all your money back, you can keep the product, and**
**I'll also pay $100 cash out of my pocket on the spot!”**

Or, even:

**“I'll TRIPLE your money back, you can keep the product, and I'll**
**even pay $100 cash out of my pocket on the spot just for wasting**
**your time!”**

If you're just starting out and want to stand out from everyone else, and
make you more “safe” to buy from, something like that can do the trick.
Especially if you have no credibility. Just make sure you fulfill on the promise,
and can handle the refunds, and that you sell something so valuable most
won't even dream of asking for a refund.
