But, what I will say is this:

Generally, I do think they’re good to have. But I don’t think you “need”
one necessarily. My highest-selling ad doesn’t have a P.S., and has never had
one since I started running it back in 2011. I don’t omit a P.S. to be contrarian
or anything — I just don’t have any reason to have one in that particular ad.

But, there are some things that make for great P.S.’s.

One of which is testimonials.

Admittedly, most of my sales copy testimonials are on a sidebar that run
along the right hand side after the product has been introduced (usually along
the bullets). But, I have also used them quite successfully in the P.S., too, such
as with my Affiliate Launch Copynomicon sales letter.

What I am not big on is...

**Inserting Testimonials**
**Into The Narrative Flow.**

You can see copywriters on the internet especially write ads where
they’re telling their story and then — out of the blue, for no real reason other
than to put a testimonial somewhere — they’ll insert a testimonial in the
middle of the story, putting the brakes on the flow of the reading. Or, almost as
bad, they’ll put a testimonial in the middle of the bullet points somewhere.

When people do that, it’s...

**Like Putting Up A Sign**
**That Says**
**“STOP READING!”**

Let’s say you’re selling someone something at your kitchen table. You’re
just talking to them, and trying to sell them something, and they’re in to your
pitch... and suddenly you say, “oh but wait a minute, I’m going to tell you what
this one guy said...” That wouldn’t make any sense then, and it makes even
less sense on a sales letter — online or offline.
