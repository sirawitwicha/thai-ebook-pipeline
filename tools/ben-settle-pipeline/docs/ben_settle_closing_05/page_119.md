**It Doesn't Come Off**
**As Manipulative Or Like A "Trick."**

More:

The takeaway — when done righteously, and as an ethic — can
potentially be more powerful than the guarantee and the bonuses combined if
you do it right. Again, there's something that drives us crazy as a species when
we're told we can't have something or why we shouldn't have something, and it
makes us want it more. Plus putting up "barriers" to a sale automatically
boosts your credibility and believability.

Like I said, it takes balls, though.

And it's hard for people who need a sale or are needy in general to do it.
But when you come at it from a customer-first point of view, and leave yourself
out of the equation, it's not only clean and ethical selling, it's also...

**Some Of The Highest**
**Level Selling There Is!**

But, only if there is a legit reason behind the takeaway.

A reason rooted in honesty and doing right by your market.

It can't be, "Ben told me to do the takeaway I'm going to use it!" and then
just insert a bunch of attitude and take-away language. Don't do that. That's
not only amateur, but it's going to make your copy sound awkward and
disingenuous. It going to hurt you more than anything, and hurt your brand
(or your client's brand) in ways you cannot even fathom in the long run. That's
why you can't be a jerk about it or throw attitude around. It's got to be matter-
of-fact, and rooted in honesty, based on you really believe certain people
should not have it.
