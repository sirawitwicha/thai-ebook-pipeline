**Close Component #3:**
**The Takeaway**

The takeaway is “sales 101”, but is easily one of the most profitable
things you can do in all your marketing. Especially since so many other
copywriters are needy — which is repulsive to would-be buyers at a gut level —
and desperately fear doing it.

Here’s what it means for sales copy:

Imagine someone reading your sales copy, they may have a little interest
or maybe even a lot of interest, but you then take it away by discouraging them
from buying or even...

**Not Letting Them**
**Buy At All!**

Why would you do this?

First as a matter of principle. Do you really think your product is a good
fit for everyone? Or are there people who should not be buying it? Such as, for
example, they would be put in financial jeopardy if they bought. Or they won’t
do anything with it. Or they are lazy and will just try to refund it anyway. Or
they are too busy to use it. And any number of other reasons.

Second, it’s psychological:

There’s something about human psychology that drives us almost stark,
raving mad when someone tells us we can’t have something. When we are told
we can’t have it, we want it even more. That may be why some of those
headlines like “Don’t read this unless” work so well. But whatever the case,
when you do a takeaway it disturbs people psychologically, knocks their sense
of skepticism off balance, and...

**Dials Up Their**
**Curiosity To 11!**
