**Close Component #1:**
**The Guarantee**

If you're selling your own products and services realize, contrary to what
a lot of guru-types say, you don't always need a guarantee. In fact I don't use
guarantees on any of my products because I don't like the kind of mindset it
gives people when I bring them in as customers. Plus, in some cases, a non-
guarantee can even help sales. However, when I have partnered in businesses
selling mass market (weight loss, golf, etc) we always used them when selling to
cold prospects.

Warm lists (i.e. our own lists) were a different matter, though. To my own
lists I'm more of a...

**“Burn The Boats”**
**Kinda Guy!**

Meaning, I want committed customers ready to take action.

I have always found them to be the best customers.

And, if I lose a few sales (to my knowledge, I haven't) because I don't have
a guarantee, the peace of mind and lack of having to deal with do-nothings and
people who are merely addicted to the dopamine drip of buying new products
with guarantees is more than worth whatever money sacrificed. Anyway, I have
a whole psychology behind this, but it's beyond the scope of this book which is
about the writing part, not the business strategy and customer-acquisition
part.

Back to the topic:

If you do use a guarantee, make it as generous and...

**Dramatic As Possible.**

So, for example, a lifetime guarantee vs a mere 30-day guarantee.
