Much of the New Testament is parables/stories that were 100% relatable and relevant to the market and people of that time. Like, for example, the parable of the good Samaritan:

**“A certain man went down from Jerusalem to Jericho, and fell among thieves, which stripped him of his raiment, and wounded him, and departed, leaving him half dead.”**

Everyone listening to that — i.e. the market — had either experienced or knew someone (or knew of someone) who had experienced being robbed on that turnpike.

It was completely relevant.

And, thus completely in their “worlds.”

As copywriter Joe Vitale wrote in his book *The 7 Lost Secrets Of Success*, if Jesus had started it off with “here’s how to be a good neighbor” it would have bored everyone. Nobody would have listened. It would not have been in their worlds. He would have been selling the market what they weren’t buying.

The stories you tell can influence and persuade like nothing else... but only if you keep them in your market’s world, by doing your research.
