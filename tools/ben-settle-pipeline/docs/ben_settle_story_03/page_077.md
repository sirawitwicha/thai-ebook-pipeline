**Storytelling Method #1:
Story About You**

This is the first and probably most obvious method.

And you simply tell a story about something that happened to you
related to your product, and tie it into what you're selling. If you ever read ads
about how somebody got started in a business or how they discovered the
solution to a vexing problem, you've seen this done before.

Origin stories are especially powerful and use this method all the time.
Plus, they are also...

**One Of Hollywood's
Most Profitable Kinds Movies.**

When Hollywood makes a trilogy, the origin story often gets the most
ticket sales because people like origin stories. They are inherently interesting
and persuasive. In fact, if you read all the ancient myths and all the ancient
fairy tales and all the legends and all the ancient religions they all have an
origin story to kick things off.

Thus, an important question to ask yourself is:

**“What’s My Origin Story?”**

(Or if you're writing copy for a client, what is their origin story.)

This is a good exercise to do even if you're not going to use it in your
sales letter. Think hard about that, and write it down, and put some thought
into it. It could be one of the single most profitable exercises you ever do, and
can be used for years and decades afterwards, not just for sales copy, but
emails, content, public speaking (should you do public speaking), and the list
goes on.
