That said, I don't want to dissuade you from using parables.

They can be extremely profitable.

Which is why in Appendix 1 in this book I analyze just such an ad in
detail that did so well the client told me when it launched they all went home
early for the day to celebrate.

The tl;dr version is this:

The product shows people fighting and self defense secrets used by inner
city cops. And I told a story about something I'd heard happen many years ago
while my dad was a cop, and that I also had seen variations of on certain police
docu-dramas. And in that story I talked about this skinny “Barney Fife” like
cop who entered a small mob of people and, within minutes, had them all on
their knees, and complying without even drawing his gun. Then, it segued not
into “and he did this using our product”, but into facts about how inner city
cops are some of the toughest around, and my client trains these people, and
in his product he shows you what he trains them. He did not say Barney used
the product, he simply used the story to illustrate why a product such as his is
something they would want.

**Storytelling Method #4: The Historical Story**

wherever else I can use them. For one thing, history has already been written

millions and millions of them, and the phrase “reality is stranger than fiction”
is absolutely true.
