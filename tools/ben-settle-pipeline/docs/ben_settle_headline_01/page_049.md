And what's the best way to get attention? To answer that question, I have

**The Secret Of The**
**"Knock Knocks"**

I was listening to an audio many years ago of the great Dan Kennedy
interviewing an old school "Mad Man" era advertising guy named Walter
Bregman. Walter had written a book called *Spray the Bear* — about his days as
an advertising guy back in the '60s. And during the interview he talked about
this idea of doing what they used to call "Knock Knocks" when he worked at an
agency called Norman B. Norman. And what they would do is pretend they'd
just been dropped into a neighborhood full of people who would make great
prospects for what they were selling. And they would pretend they knocked on
the door, and ask themselves:

**"What can you say in 6 or 7 words when the person opens the door**
**and, instead of slamming the door shut on you, they grab you by**
**the arm, yank you inside, and demand you tell them more?"**

I am paraphrasing a bit (about them grabbing your arm and dragging
you in, but that is how I approach it).

But imagine you're going door-to-door to door in the most hostile selling
environment there is (door-to-door) and instead of somebody racking a shot
gun at you and then telling you to get the hell off their lawn...

**They're Practically Kidnapping You**
**And Making You Tell Them More**
**About What You're Selling!**

That's what your headline should ideally do.
