**3. The Emotion Headline**

This is one of my favorite headlines to use, and also goes back to market
research and knowing what their fears are and what their desires are and what
they want.

Like the market-oriented headline, this does not make a claim.

You're simply taking the strongest emotion your market has, and relate it
to what you sell. It's also another kind of headline you probably won't see a lot
of people other than really high level copywriters use. This is similar to the last
headline where I started with the word "Warning", but this was for the golf
market:

**"Warning: Do not read this**
**unless you are frustrated as hell**
**with your golf swing!"**

There isn't a golfer in that market who isn't frustrated with his golf
swing. At least, not the segment we targeted. And so, it lit up the prospect's
psychology like a Christmas tree.

Remember, the answer is always in your market. And this is especially
true when you are thinking up headlines. Tap into a strong emotion (or, as the
great copywriter Clayton Makepeace says, their "dominant resident emotion")
and it's extremely difficult for them to ignore you and not keep reading.

**4. The Blatant Offer Headline**

These can be great if you're selling something people already know they
want, or it's a benefit they already know they crave, and you just happen to
have an offer that's going to rock their world.

It is the most straightforward way of doing a headline.
