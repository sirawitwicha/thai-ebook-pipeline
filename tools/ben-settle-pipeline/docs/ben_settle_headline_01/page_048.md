Starting in this chapter, we’re going to go through the 5-step Copy
Slacker formula in detail. As I mentioned earlier, there is nothing complicated
about it. I broke the process down into its simplest form for my own sake many
years ago. The result has been writing sales letters and other copy much faster,
and with less overwhelm and frustration.

Plus, I got immediately higher response, too.

The five steps are headline, opening paragraph, story, bullets, close. And
the first step — the headline — is by far the most valuable piece of “real estate”
in your entire ad. The great ad man David Ogilvy — who spent probably tens of
millions of dollars in newspaper advertising for himself and for his clients —
says your headline is 80% of the power of your ad, and I doubt many (or any)
other copywriter would dispute that. In fact, Gene Schwartz said if you are
competing against an amateur copywriter but his headline is way better than
yours, you’ll probably lose even if the rest of your ad is “better.”

With that said, the first thing to realize is...

**It’s Not The**
**Headline’s Job To Sell.**

It can sell, in some cases.
