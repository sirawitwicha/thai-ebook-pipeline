Gene Schwartz once analyzed some of these headlines and talked about

**8. The Straight Benefit Headline**

This is related to the blatant offer headline. But in this case you're
coming out and stating a big benefit. These aren't working as well as they
probably did back in the day when advertising was new. But they can still work
if you're solving a strong and urgent problem.

Here's an example:

**“Get Up to 12 Miles More Per Gallon From Your Car”**

That ad was written during a time when gas prices was a big thing on
people's minds. No twists, turns, or tricks needed in that case.

**9. The Question Headline**

I once hard the great Sean D'Souza say it is neurologically impossible for
someone to ignore a question that's interesting to them.

(i.e. it's in their “world”)

You can't not notice it. It's going to make you at least pause, and there's
nothing hypey about these headlines. You're just asking a question. I'm not a
lawyer (nor do I play one in a copywriting book), but from what I understand,
you can get away with making a lot of claims the government would never let
you make in an ad simply by putting a question mark at the end of it.

Check with a lawyer with that, of course.
