There are two formulas I've heard relevant to this:

First is the 40/40/20 rule — meaning 40% of the success of your sales
letter is based on the list or visitors to your site, i.e. the quality of your
prospect. Another 40% is what you're offering them. And then 20% is your
sales copy. The second formula (that I like better) is 50% is your list/traffic,
30% offer, 20% copy. Either way, your copy is 20% and the offer is a bigger
deciding factor whether someone buys or not. So if you have a great offer, you
can try doing a headline with just that.

**"Now You Can Try**
**My Entire Swing Training System**
**And Hot New Driver Video For Just $1"**

want what you have.

**5. The Contrast Headline**

It's hard to not notice contrasting ideas because they tap right into
someone's neurology via their sense of curiosity. In fact, I'll give you a little side
note here about the late, great copywriting and marketing genius, Gary
Halbert. I once talked to his protégé, the late Scott Haines, and Scott told me
how one day Gary took him on his boat. Gary apparently liked doing this to let
his mind get ideas. And he asked Scott to tell him what the most important
appeal of advertising is. And Scott said, "That's easy, it's self interest." And
Gary Halbert told him no, it's not self interest...

**It's Curiosity!**

He then talked about how people will buy very expensive products due
just to curiosity, and having an itch in their brains they must scratch. It's why
people will buy a very expensive product because of a single bullet point buried
on page 8 of a 20-page ad — that bullet just "got" them.
