**11. “Combine & Conquer” Headline**

This is where you mix & match the prior ten headlines we just talked
about with each other. The more of these headlines you write, the more natural
this gets, and the more success you should see.

So for example:

**“When Doctors Have Headaches What Do They Do?”**

I believe John Caples wrote this one. It combines proof with a question,
and it killed a pure question headline he tested it against.

Here’s another one

**“An Open Letter to Readers of Black Belt Magazine Who Are Fed Up With**
**the Hype Surrounding the Seemingly Endless String of Fighter of the**
**Month Programs”**

That’s a market oriented headline mixed with an emotional headline. I
remember reading that and thinking it brilliant. I don’t know how well this
headline did, but it’s a good example of combining two headline types. When
you do this, you’re tapping into the market and what they want and what
they’re thinking about and an emotion. It’s very powerful.
