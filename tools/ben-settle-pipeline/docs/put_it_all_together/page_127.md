**Take One Bite**
**At A Time!**

Don't let the 10+ pages (or however much research you have — the more
the better) overwhelm you. Just start the timer, and start massaging whatever
stands out. When you get bored of one thing, move to another section and see
if something stands out. When that bores you, move to another, and so on and
so forth. Then, when that timer goes off, you stop working for 5 or 10 minutes.
I don't recommend checking email or social media or your phone or anything
that is going to suck you into a black hole. Just stand up, stretch, pet your dog
or take your dog out, hit the floor and pound out some pushups, make yourself
a cup of tea, or whatever it is.

Then, push start on the timer and get back at it.

Aim for at least four 33:33 sessions per day at first. That works out to a
little over 2 solid hours of work. Real work. Not “busy” work. I remember
hearing John Carlton say just working two hours per day can move mountains
and he was absolutely right.
