Realize this:

You cannot educate someone enough about a problem that effects their
lives and/or causes them pain. The more you can create that vision of what
their life will be like if they don't fix whatever problem plagues them, the more
willing they will often be to not only tolerate but act upon your pitch to buy
from you. This goes back to being in their "world." What are their worst pains,
insecurities, nightmares? What do they lay at night thinking about
staring holes in the ceiling worrying about? You can talk about those problems
as much as you want, in some cases. Get detailed, get gory (figuratively
speaking), and get specific. But what you don't want to do is give them the
solutions in the product. And if you do, be vague and incomplete. The 90/10
solution is perfect for this — which is often just telling people "what" to do to
solve a problem or get what they want, but now "how" to do it. For those
details, they have to buy.

If you want to see some great examples of this, use the internet to seek
out John Carlton ads in the fitness and self defense market (the ones that ran
in magazines). A lot of Gary Halbert ads did this, too. And, a lot of magalogs
approach their copy this way, as well.

Best part is, a lot of these ads are found online, free.

Worst case, ask the copywriters directly.
