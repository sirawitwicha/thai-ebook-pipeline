**You Will Be Astonished**
**By How Fast You Write Your Copy!**

One of the best parts of this “one 33:33 bite at a time” process is, you
don’t get all freaked out by all this information in front of you. Your goal isn’t to
write the ad, it’s to massage for 33:33 the way I described.

And that’s what you do:

Take that hot, steamy mess of words and start massaging.

One sentence at a time... one paragraph at a time... one page at a time...
skipping over to another section whenever you get bored, and keeping
everything in its rightful sections. It won’t be long until your brain gets into the
act and starts telling you, “Ooh this look like it could be simplified...” “Oh
that’s kind of an interesting idea, let me play with that...” In other words, don’t
think of it as “work” or even “copywriting” at all, just think of it as a puzzle or a
game you get paid for when you’re finished.

During this process you’re doing what Hemingway described as:

**“Writing Drunk”**

Not literally drunk, of course.

**It means you write without inhibition.**

There are no blockages between your brain to your fingertips while you’re
typing. You’re writing with the door closed as Stephen King calls it in his book
*On Writing*. He talks about his first draft not being fit for reading by anyone,
and only for his eyes behind closed doors. During this phase nobody is looking
over your shoulder, no client is judging it, and nobody is going to see it. It’s just
you and the market and the words. And before long (even in the very first day,
when you get good at it) you will have a first draft. It still won’t be ready to be
run, or even read by anyone else. But you’ll have your headline. You’ll have
your opening paragraph. You’ll have your story, your bullets, and your close.

At that point, take another break (for the day, ideally, unless you’re
under a looming deadline) and then start doing what Hemingway also
described as:

**“Edit Sober”**
