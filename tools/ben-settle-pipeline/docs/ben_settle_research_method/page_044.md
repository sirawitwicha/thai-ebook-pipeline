If you're selling your own product or service, and if you really want to get

**Hire A Journalist**
**To Interview You!**

I've never done this myself.

But, I would do this if I had to, and if I couldn't find anyone else to do it.
Journalists are trained to extract info. And it can even just be a good
journalism student, who will probably work cheap or even free just for the
experience. Try and get them to stump you, pick out your product or service's
flaws, and be as cynical as they can, just like a skeptical customer will. That
way they challenge you on it, you uncover objections you may not have
otherwise thought of, and you could even get ideas on how to improve your
product or service.

Another thing you can do is what I call:

**"Dog Leash Research"**

This is a bizarre (but ridiculously effective) way of using an ordinary dog
leash to create ads that can win even against better "written" copy.

Here's how it works:

Besides just transcribing your client or yourself talking about the
product and market... you keep the audio, too, and load it onto your MP3
player. And what I would do is go walk my dog for an hour or two every day,
and just listen to it, constantly imbibing the product and the client's mindset,
and everything behind that product and the market we discussed. Doing this
helped me beat a lot of copywriters I know who had more talent than me. If you
don't have a dog, simply take a walk by yourself. There is something about
listening to something over and over — 10 times or more — that seeps
everything into your brain and psychology. And when you sit down to write
your advertising, it all flows out in ways you'd never see otherwise.

**More Product Research Ideas**

I've already mentioned looking at your competitors' products on the
market research side. But you can also do this on the product side, too. Look
at their ads, the benefits they're using, and the hooks they're using.
