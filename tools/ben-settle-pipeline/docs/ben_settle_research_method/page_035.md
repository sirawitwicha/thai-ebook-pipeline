**45. How aware are your prospects of the problem your product solves on a
scale of 1 to 10?**

The late, great Gene Schwartz's magnificent book *Breakthrough
Advertising* goes into great detail about market awareness.

(And I highly recommend you get it.)

A lot of ads fail not because the copy is bad, but because they don't know
the awareness level their market has of the problem they are solving.
Sometimes people don't even realize they have the problem and have to be
shown they have the problem your product or service solves. Others know they
have the problem and are looking for a solution. In some cases, the market is
already aware not only of the problem your product solves but wants your
product to solve it (the easiest sale to make). Take someone who's buying
Viagra. They're already aware of their problem. They already are looking for
Viagra. They don't need a big sales pitch. On the other hand, someone who just
knows there is something wrong with their foot, but are in research mode, is a
much tougher sell. They can't even necessarily verbalize the problem making
that a different sales pitch.
