**36. What TV shows are they watching?**

In the self defense niche, I knew what TV shows my market was watching because we did this thing called a Claritas Report (which I'll talk about more later) on his buyers, and we found out many of them watched the show "24." Thus, in one of the ads, I referenced the main character Jack Bauer to enhance the story I told in the copy. It may sound hokey, but in the context of the ad it was great. And, incidentally, you can read the ad in the swipe file at the end of this book.

**37. Do they crave recognition from anyone?**

A lot of people crave recognition from someone. They may or may not admit it, which is why you have to dig it out. But when you find out who your market craves recognition from, you might be able to use that in your copy.

**38. Who is it they want to impress the most?**

Whose "radar" do they want to be on? A woman? Their boss? Friends? Spouse? Kids? Customers? A coach?

And, what do they want from those they wish to impress?

**39. What do they do for fun?**

Do they like to go to the ballgame or do they just like to sit around and watch TV? Do they like to go hiking or biking? Or is their idea of fun fishing, hunting, or target shooting? Maybe they like to play basketball or hit museums.

The key is to find out what they actually do, and not say they like to do.

If you ask certain people if they prefer going to the opera or the movies, they may say opera just to sound smart, but have only gone once in their lives. If someone says they prefer reading the Bible over reading popular fiction, chances are they have read more Stephen King books than Bible verses. In my area, there are a lot of people who like to talk about how passionate they are about hiking, but have only gone once in the last year.

This is why observing what they do and buy is better than asking.

And, this is why you have to dig this info out, usually.
