**28. What is the number one most urgent hot button problem they're attempting to solve right now?**

If you can narrow your product or service down to one overarching and urgent problem it solves, that they want solved now, then 80% of your job is done before you write a single word of copy. It can shape your headline, your story, your bullets, and everything else.

This is easier for some markets (i.e. health) than others.

But, this is why research is so vital to do.

And, also, why it's so profitable when done right...

**29. What feelings are they experiencing when they're doing a Google search or engaging in whatever media you use to generate your leads?**

How are they feeling when they put in a keyword and then your site pops up, or when they click a link in an email, or when they hear your URL mentioned, or when they open up an envelope and see your sales copy?

The answer could have a huge impact on your headline, especially.

**30. How do you want the visitor to feel when they arrive on your site or see your sales letter?**

All decisions — especially buying decisions — are made on emotion, not logic. In fact, the decision to be “logical” is itself an emotional decision. That's why answering this question, and building your sales pitch around it, is so important.

**31. What do they secretly fantasize about having the most in life?**

It could be a strong body that makes women swoon over them. It could be having a giant house and a boat or living on a boat in the middle of the ocean and never having to deal with people. Or it could be having fame, fortune, power, money, or confidence.

Everybody fantasizes about something.
