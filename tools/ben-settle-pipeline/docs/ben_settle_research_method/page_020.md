I was originally inspired to think this way a while back when thinking
about how certain law enforcement agents get inside the heads of the criminals
they track down. They don’t ask these exact questions we’re going to cover here
for writing copy obviously, but it’s the attitude they take that’s important, and
the way they go about gathering info. They want to crawl inside the villain’s
mind, anticipate their next move, understand their motivations, and be
positioned to be waiting for the bad guy when he makes that move.

I call it:

**“Criminally Profitable Research”**

Not because your prospects are literally criminals.

But because by using this approach, you can dig up exactly what you
need to say, what you need to demonstrate, and what you need to prove your
case to them to influence them to buy from you, ignore your competition, and
keep their attention even while being bombarded by ringing phones, emails,
breaking news, text messages, social media alerts, crying babies, ringing
doorbells, slamming doors, and an infinite number of other things trying to
steal their attention away from you.

Do this right and you can have the kind of positioning where, even if
your copy is not the “best”, it’ll resonate with your readers and easily be good
enough to still make a lot of sales...

**With Happy Customers**
**Who Are Eager To Buy!**

And the key to doing this is interacting with your market.

That may seem obvious.

But it is astounding how few copywriters bother to do it — relying
instead on their opinions, projections, emotions, surveys, and anything else
that isn’t interacting with their customer, by talking to them (yes, in “real life”)
going into their forums and social media groups, observing what they’re
already spending money on (the best kind of “survey” there is...), looking at the
sales letters and advertising they bought from, secret shopping those
competitors (yes, you buy what the market is buying), and any other way you
can experience what they experience what they experience, feel what they feel,
and live the lives they live.
