**40. What do they complain about to their friends over drinks?**
Imagine someone in your market — the ideal customer — at the bar
talking to his best friend about whatever the problem is your product solves.

How does he explain it?

What does he say?

What exact way does he phrase it?

What are his emotions when he says it, what's the look on his face,
what's the tone in his voice?

**41. What roles do they play in life?**
Are they a boss, a parent, a lover, a spouse? Is it a leader, coach, adviser,
or teammate? Is it banker (they pay for some deadbeat), cleaning lady (clean up
after their boyfriend), or chauffeur (they drive someone around all the time)?

What are their roles?

**42. Are they looking to be sold by the time they find you, or are they
going to be looking for more information?**
The answer will change the way you write your ad copy in multiple ways.
Such as, will you have to make it look more like a report than a pitch.

**43. What other related products are they buying?**
This can not only affect your sales copy, but may give you some great
ideas for up-sells, too.

**44. Do they hang out in any forums or chat rooms you can lurk at?**
If they do, go there and lurk and study and imbibe everything everyone
says. Every story, every complaint, every solution, every brain fart — everything
they talk about when they think what they are saying is "anonymous." I have
found on these sites people will say things they would never say to someone in
person, including their best friends, parents, or even their spouse.
