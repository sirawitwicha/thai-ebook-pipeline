The good news about getting into your prospect's world is... all you have
to do to get there is ask the right questions, make intelligent observations, and
find out what they are already spending money on — and not what they merely
say they want. Do that and writing ads that make your business sales can
become almost as routine as writing a grocery list.

But if you do it wrong, and you're pulling the proverbial feed sack string
down the middle, you might make some sales, but most of them will spill out
all over the place, and...

**You'll Leave A Lot Of Profit
On The Table!**

Take, for example, The Gap.

They learned this the hard way several years ago.

I read in the great Ken McCarthy's book *The System Club Letters* how
many years ago they started relying on surveys (what their customers said they
wanted, not what they were buying) and they took a huge dump in sales. This
is a company that can afford to hire the best researchers in the business, and
they blew it so bad I am not sure they ever fully recovered from it. Either way,
it was very telling.
