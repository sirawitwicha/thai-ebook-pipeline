**Completely “Blind.”**

This may sound “back-asswards” — but this is how old school
copywriters wrote copy that crawled inside their customer’s psychology and
virtually tormented them until they bought. In fact, many years ago, I was
listening to a series of calls about the late, great copywriter Gene Schwartz.
Many master copywriters were on these telephone calls, including the late Milt
Pierce. He was the great Bob Bly’s first teacher, and a brilliant guy. And he said
they would write the ad first, without seeing the product. He said they would
pretend they had God-like unlimited powers to grant the market whatever the
market wanted. Then, they would write the ad promising all those benefits.
Next, they would create the product from there, and try to fulfill as many of
those benefits as possible. Finally, they’d remove the claims from the ad they
couldn’t fulfill and run the ad. These ads would sell like crazy, and it’s the ideal
way to do it. Instead of creating the product and then the ad, you create the ad
and then build the product around that.

But it only works if you have the market research down pat. Remember,
it’s not about the weedkiller.
