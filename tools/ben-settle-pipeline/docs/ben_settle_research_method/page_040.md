**The Other Side**
**Of The Bridge**

Now we're going to talk about the other side of the "bridge" Doug D'Anna talked about — the product side. The market research is one side, the product is the other.

Good news is, the product research is a lot more straightforward.

The first thing to do when researching your product or service is:

**Stuff Your Brain!**

The late copywriter Scott Haines called this "circling the desk." To do this you want to know your product or your service or whatever it is your selling — whether it's your own or it's for somebody else — better than anyone else does. If it's a book or informational product you ideally want to know that product better than the author. What I like to do is go through an informational product at least ten times. I know time can be a factor. But the more times you go through something, the better you're going to know it, and the deeper your knowledge will be. Yes, there is nothing "slackerish" about the research-side. That comes with the writing part. But the foundation of anything you want to build always takes the most time. And sales copy is no different.
