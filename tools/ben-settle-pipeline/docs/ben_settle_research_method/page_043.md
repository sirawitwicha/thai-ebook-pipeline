Here's another tip, that's kind of sneaky:

**Look At Your Competitor's**
**Product Or Service Testimonials!**

I have gotten many ad copy ideas from reading competing product
testimonials. They sometimes contain market insights, product claims that
apply to my product (that I hadn't thought of), and ideas for headlines, claims,
benefits, and bullets. It's like getting other copywriters to help you sell more of
your products without them even knowing about it.

You just want to absorb everything you can.

Write every idea you get down — big or small.

And, go through all those notes and ideas several times.

You should also talk to the product creator or inventor or whoever
provides the service (if you're selling a service). If it's your own product or
service, have somebody interview you. Have them ask you questions and force
you to think about it because you're so close to your own product you may
have forgotten things. But either way, you want to interview the product
creator and get them telling you stories. Lots and lots of stories. Stories about
what made them create it. What inspired it. Who it's helped and how. And the
list goes on. Make sure you record everything (get permission, of course). But
you want them uninhibited — telling you stories in their own language and
using their own language.

This is especially important if you're writing for a client because now
you'll have notes and transcriptions, and it will all...

**Be In The Client's**
**Exact Voice!**

You'll have their words, their exact way of phrasing things, their
personality, their vernacular... and when you put that in an ad they're not
going to say "oh this doesn't sound like me!", it's going to sound just like them.
I've had clients say, "It sounds just like me talking! How did you do this?" and
the answer is, it was all them. If you're a freelancer, this is going to get less
resistance to them running your ad too.
