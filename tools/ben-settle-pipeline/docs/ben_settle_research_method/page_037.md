This is not the end-all-be-all solution for market research.

And, you have to be careful you're not studying someone's bad market
research. But other ads in your market are good beacons for getting some of
the answers to the market research questions above.

- **Google & social media analytics**

Any platform that uses pay-per-click can give you tremendous insights
into your market. In some cases, you can find out even the really obscure
questions like what they are reading and watching. I don't pretend to be an
expert on the technicalities of how to do this. But any decent user of these
platforms can help you.

- **Vintage sales letters**

Sometimes — especially if you are selling a mass market, like weight
loss, etc — you can get tremendous insights reading older ads. Those old
school copywriters had a way of creeping right into your psychology and
knowing exactly what to say to get people to buy.

Think of it this way:

The really older ads were selling products to leads who didn't even know
the product existed 5 minutes earlier, without ease-of-order solutions like
credit cards, PayPal, etc. In some cases, they may have lived 10 miles from a
post office, had to make the trek into town, get a money order, then go to the
post office and mail their order in.

There are many places you can find vintage direct response (very
important they are direct response ads, and not image ads) ads. Just go to
Google and it will show you all kinds. Also, old comic book ads are great for
this, too.

- **Surveys**

You can learn a lot about what your market responds to by finding out
what sales copy they are currently buying from. If you have your own list, you
can ask them what the most recent product on your topic they bought was.
