**32. What kind of slang do they use in everyday conversation?**

One of the best ways to create trust and credibility in your sales copy is
to talk "like" your market. The more you sound like one of them, the more
likely they are to buy.

And, this is especially true of knowing what slang they use.

What figures of speech do they use.

What unique jargon do they use.

If you don't sound like them, you're going to be looked at as an outsider
and nobody wants to buy from an outsider.

**33. Who else is successfully selling something similar to them and how?**

This goes back to checking out your competition and reading their ads.
Look at what's working, what's not working. Then, see how you can do yours
better, or possibly completely differently if you see them making mistakes due
to lack of doing their own research.

**34. What books are they reading?**

The books your market is reading can have a huge bearing on how you
write your ads and the appeals you make and what they like and what
entertains them. They can also give you topics to touch on in your copy.

**35. What movies are they watching?**

Are these people who just like to watch romance and chick flicks or are
these people who won't go to a movie theater unless there's a Hobbit or a
lightsaber or a dragon or a superhero in there? Maybe they like horror films, or
maybe they only watch foreign films with subtitles.

Again, this may have zero bearing on your copy directly.

But, the more you know your customer, the better.
