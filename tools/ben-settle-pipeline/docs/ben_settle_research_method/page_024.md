**What Are They**
**Utterly Terrified Of?**

Are they worried about their children being kidnapped in their rapidly-
deteriorating neighborhood? Are they worried about their parents dying before
finding out they betrayed them? Are they worried about the economy going to
hell and they have no savings to speak of? Are they worried about being blown
up by a terrorist? Are they worried about the floating debris they see in their
drinking water? Are they worried their favorite supplement is going to be
discontinued on Amazon and they don't know where else to get it? Does their
neighborhood being quickly gentrified worry them? Are they worried their
political party will lose power in the next election?

Everyone worries about something.

And the more of those worries you know, the better, even if those worries
have nothing directly to do with what you're selling.

**9. What are their values like?**

One big mistake copywriters make is projecting their values onto their
market. To get in your market's world you must temporarily become them. And
you can do that by finding out what their values are, understanding them (even
if you don't agree with them), and making sure your copywriting doesn't violate
them. Nothing will get your copy ignored or deleted faster than projecting your
values onto them.

For example:

I may think the so-called "Global Warming" scare is a politically-charged
theory full of fraud and lies, but if my market buys into it, the last thing I'm
going to do in a sales letter is mock or marginalize that fear they have. Same
with atheists belittling religion, conservatives hating on liberals, career women
mocking housewives, and the list goes on.
