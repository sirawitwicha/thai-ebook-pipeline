Make sure you research the product creator or the author or whoever it
is that created the product or service. You can find all kinds of powerful bits of
information you can use to sell with.

For example:

I once wrote a sales letter for a product about copywriting, co-taught by
David Garfinkel. David Garfinkel was the star of that ad, he had the
positioning, name appeal, etc. I had been studying David Garfinkel already,
and there was an interview he did with the late copywriter John Ritz. It was a
great interview and David said something in that recording that had nothing to
do with the product I was selling at all. There was no relation to it whatsoever
to what I was selling. But he said something in that interview about an
advantage he has when it comes to writing headlines. Again, this is not
mentioned at all in the product I was selling. But, I was able to use the fact
that he was the Bureau Chief for one of San Francisco's major newspapers,
and that experience gave him headline writing skills most copywriters simply
don't have. That one little fact made it into a bullet point as proof and
credibility to help sell my product. Again, I had my "antenna" up doing
research. You can get many ideas from studying the creator of the product.
Including ideas that have nothing to do directly with the product, but that...
