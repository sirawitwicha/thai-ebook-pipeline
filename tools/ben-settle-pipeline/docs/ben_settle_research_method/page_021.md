Following are some questions to ask your market I make consulting
clients (who have paid me $15k a pop) fill out so I could better help them. It
may not be possible to get the answers to all of them. And it may not always be
as simple as asking (this is where observation comes in). But the more of these
questions you can get answers to, the faster and easier your sales copy will be
to write. The more sales your copy will make. And, the bigger your business (or
your client's business, if'n you do client work...) will grow.

So here are the questions:

**1. What keeps them awake at night?**

What plagues them with Sunday night insomnia and has them staring
holes in the ceiling while everyone else is sleeping like a baby?

What do they roll over and worry about to their spouse in regards to
what your product or service can help them with? For example, I had one client
who sells to real estate agents who struggle to get leads. They (literally —
according to him) come home after a hard day of work, stressed to the hilt, over
leveraged on their spending and in debt, and say, “I really need to find a way to
get leads...”

Which, incidentally, formed the headline and theme of my ad.

Point is this:

This question alone — “what keeps them up at night?” — will give you so
much sales copy fodder it won't be a matter of what to include in your copy,
but what to leave out...

**2. What are the top things they are terrified of, listed in order?**

Think of it as their own “house of horrors.” What things do they readily
admit and are obvious, and also things they are too ashamed to admit. These
are often easily dug up, too just by asking and observing. In the weight loss
niche, for example, I discovered how terrified my prospects were of being asked
when the baby is due.

An irrational fear to many people. But most of our fears (yes, even yours,
Chuckles) are irrational.
