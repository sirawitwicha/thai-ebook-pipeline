**25. Where are they located?**

What part of the country or what part of the world do they live in and what's that like? What's their town like? What are their friends like? What kind of people hang out in their location? Is it in a gated community here or a low rent apartment building? Dan Kennedy once talked about driving a neighborhood with one of his carpet cleaning clients. They discovered the client was trying to sell expensive carpet cleaning to people who either had little money or didn't care what their carpets looked like.

Your market's location matters — even if you're selling online.

**26. Do they own a home or do they rent?**

Is it expensive, is it cheap? Is it nice, or is it dirty? What's their landlord like? Again, some of these questions are going to be hard to answer.

I'm just trying to get you in the right mindset.

This is why you want to go out and talk to people in your market whenever you can, especially buyers if you have any yet, and drive around where they live whenever possible.

**27. What are the biggest mistakes your prospects are making that your product can help them stop making?**

If you sell a product or service, that means you are solving problems. And many of those problems are very likely being causes by mistakes your market is making. That is why this list should be as detailed as possible — and the answers can make for excellent sales copy in everything from sales letters (text or video), to emails, podcasts, content, public speaking, and everything in between.
