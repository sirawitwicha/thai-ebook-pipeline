**10. What religion are they?**

Speaking of values... what religion are they? Christian? Muslim? Jewish?
Buddhist? Mormon? Or are they mostly irreligious, atheist, or agnostic? If
you're writing to hardcore Christians, for example, you're going to approach
your copy differently than you would an Atheist.

**11. What's their most urgent hot button crisis right now?**

Many people live crisis-to-crisis. For example, if you write in the financial
market, your prospect's most urgent problem today could be completely
different tomorrow depending on what just happened in the stock market 4
minutes ago, what they read in the newspaper, what the weather is like half
way around the world, or who just got elected or is under investigation.

What is the crisis in their life right now?

And, how does your product or service affect it?

**12. Who are their enemies?**

Everybody has enemies or perceived enemies — and sometime you can
rail against them in your advertising if you know who they are. Their enemy
can be anything from the office politician at work trying to get them fired... to a
bureaucrat fining them... to a doctor who won't prescribe them more meds... to
a lazy mailman who doesn't deliver their mail and returns everything to
sender... to a child's teacher who refuses to give that child a fair grade, and the
list goes on.

It can also be a politician.

A former spouse, boyfriend, or girlfriend.

A social media troll always dogging them.

Or a mother-in-law trying to sabotage their marriage, a landlord raising
their rent, a crooked JV partner who won't pay them, a buddy always trying to
jinx their golf game, a waitress always flirting with their boyfriend, and the list
goes on.

Know thy market's enemies, and you'll know far more sales...
