**22. What's their education level?**

Are they high school dropouts or high school grads?

Did they go to college? If so, do they have a PhD? A Master's Degree? A
community college degree?

Or are they self taught?

Were they home schooled? Were they in private school or did they go to
public school? Know as much as you can about that because it's going to
matter. Many copywriters "dumb down" their language depending on the
market, and that is often a good idea. Not because they are dumb, but because
most people read at a 5th grade level. The late, great Gene Schwartz used to
advise keeping your words exactly at 3 or less syllables when writing to a mass
market. On the other hand, the late, great Jim Rutz said he didn't worry about
that when writing in the financial market. In his way of thinking, if they have
enough money to invest they are probably smarter than he is.

This is why you have to know your market.

Even something as trivial as using a word or not using a word that could
go over a prospect's head can make the difference between making a lot of
extra sales or just a trickle of extra sales, or when deciding to use text or video,
or if you want to attract a certain kind of buyer over another.

**23. Are they in debt?**

Are they maxed out on credit cards? How much debt are they in? Do they
own their house free and clear, or are they upside down on their mortgage? Do
they pay cash for everything, or do they not even worry about the balance on
their credit card? Do they despise debt or are they comfortable with it? These
things can shape the way you create and present your offer.
