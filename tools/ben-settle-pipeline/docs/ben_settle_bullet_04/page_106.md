**Bullet Point Secret #8:**
**The Giveaway**

This is another one I learned from observing John Carlton, and also
certain copywriters' magalogs and other pieces. And this is where you give
away an entire secret or the whole tip right in a bullet point — with no tease or
hiding what it is whatsoever. It's all out there in the open, and it can do
wonders for...

**Proving You're**
**Not A Charlatan!**

After all, you're giving the entire tip away. They can test it for themselves
to see if you're full of nonsense or not. This is especially potent for
informational products, but you can do it selling services and other products,
too.

But, a caution:

I highly recommend you only use these once in a while. The point isn't to
try to look like a nice and generous guy, it's to demonstrate your knowledge so
they can be confident you know what you're talking about, and to make all the
other bullets (especially the more outrageous ones) sound more believable:

-   **Nausea — settle a queasy stomach with a bowl of milk toast. Amazing! Page 5**
-   **How to use simple cartoons to explode your sales... no matter what you sell or who you sell to! (For example, you'd be amazed at how easily a picture of a phone operator with her headset on can jack up your response!)**

Notice how they're simple, to the point, and give away the whole tip. They
give the reader confidence that you know what you're talking about, and a
chance for them to prove it on their own, if they wish.
