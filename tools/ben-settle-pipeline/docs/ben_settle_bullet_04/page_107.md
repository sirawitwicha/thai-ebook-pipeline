Enter the 90% solution bullet.

This is where you give away 90% of the secret in the bullet, but leave off the 10% they need to implement whatever that secret is. David likened it to baiting a fishing hook — where you put just enough bait to get the fish to bite, and not the entire can of worms. Doing this not only creates overwhelming curiosity, but it also gives you credibility to draw the reader in even more.

Here is one example I wrote in the weight loss niche:

- **Why you should never chew gum if you are trying to lose weight!**

In that bullet, I'm telling you never to chew gum but I'm not really telling you why you shouldn't, and I'm not telling you if there are any exceptions to the rule, because a lot of people who are trying to lose weight are chewing gum. This creates a bit of that chaos and inconsistency in their heads if they've been told to chew gum to lose weight, and ups the chances of them buying to get that answer.

Here's another example written by Gary Halbert:

- **The real reason why Prozac and Zoloft are so popular in this country! (Almost no one... not even doctors... understands the startling sexual implications.)**

A full 90% of the secret is there (Prozac and Zoloft have sexual implications). But, you don't know why, how, or the details. Do you just start popping these drugs to start popping erections? Or is there more to it? There's only one way to find out — and that's to get the product.
