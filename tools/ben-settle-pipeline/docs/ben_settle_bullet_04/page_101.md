I first heard about this kind of bullet from the great Gene Schwartz, and it's a "child" of the curiosity bullet, but it's a little bit different at the same time. It's when you make a benefit or a claim or something about your product that is *almost* unbelievable.

In other words, it's not impossible. Or outside the bounds of reality. But it is right at the cusp of reality, maybe a foot on the other side, and when you do that correctly...

**It Practically Forces People**
**To Want To Respond To Your Offer**
**Just To Get The Answer!**

Here's an example I wrote for an ad to MLM people who wanted to learn how to get traffic:

- A secret, "back door" way of getting Google to reduce your pay-per-click fees... without even logging into your AdWords account.

To people in that market, they had to wonder how is that even possible and it got their attention. Here's another one Gene Schwartz talked about in a training he once gave to Rodale Books:

- How to vacuum, dust, and clean your dresser drawer without removing its contents!

Again, it's not impossible, but *almost* impossible. And it drives people batty wanting to know the answer if you are talking to the market about what's important to them.

To use these, simply think of the things your product or service does and then write a bullet in such a way where it sounds almost impossible but not completely impossible.
