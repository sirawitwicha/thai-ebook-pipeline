**Bullet Point Secret #7:**
**Tell Them What NOT To Do**

When you write a bullet telling someone what not to do or a mistake
they're making or something they shouldn't do it's inherently interesting, and
is almost guaranteed to get their attention if they are doing or engaging in or
thinking of doing the very thing you're telling them not to do:

-   **The #1 WORST mistake you can make with your wrist when
    swinging a golf club. (Even if you do everything else "right",
    your game will fall apart if you make this common mistake
    nobody talks about.)**

-   **Four words that will destroy your chances of sponsoring new
    reps — even the ones who already want to sign up! (When I
    stopped saying these four words — that 99% of network
    marketers say during calls — my sponsoring rate shot through
    the roof.)**

These are curiosity-laden and, when you do them right (especially if you
can insert specifics — i.e. "four words", etc), difficult for people to ignore, while
not coming off as the usual hype-and-nonsense claims a lot of bullets they see
sound like.
