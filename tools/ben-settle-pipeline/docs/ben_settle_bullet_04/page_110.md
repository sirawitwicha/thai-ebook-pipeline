**More Bullet Point Ammo**

-   **Beef up your bookends** — I once heard the great Dan Kennedy talk
    about putting your best and most persuasive bullets as the first and the
    last bullet. This makes a lot of sense when you realize there are
    skimmers who will begin by reading the first bullet, and then scroll down
    to the last bullet, before deciding to read more of them. This way you are
    more likely to hook them by getting them in the two spots they are most
    likely to read with your very best bullets.

-   **Use the loop effect** — If you have a ton of bullets (I have an ad with 200
    bullets, where I did this) do an expansion bullet every 10 or 15 bullets,
    depending on how many you have. So for example, I'll do an expansion
    bullet every ten or fifteen bullets, and then after that expansion bullet I
    get them right back into another list of ten or twenty bullets followed by
    another expansion bullet, and so on and so forth. My pal and copywriter
    John Angelhache saw me do this when we were in a mastermind group
    together, and basically called it an almost hypnotic-like “loop effect”
    drawing him deeper into the copy. And it's worked well every time I've
    done it.

-   **Vary up your ammo** — by using as many of the above kinds of bullets
    as you can. You may find yourself gravitating toward certain bullets you
    like using the most (I do), but the more you can mix them up the better
    to keep peoples' lagging attention spans off balance. In Wing Chun Kung
    Fu it's all about keeping your opponent off balance. Take their balance
    and you take their center of gravity. Take the center of gravity and you
    all but render someone powerless against you in a fight. Even if they do
    hit you, it'll be weak and harmless. And when you use lots of different
    kinds of bullets keep people psychologically off balance as far as them
    getting bored or comfortable. You want there to constantly be tension
    going on, whether you're using curiosity on them or using some proof on
    them or you're giving a whole bullet away and then the next one you're
    teasing them with a 90/10 solution bullet... and you're creating all this
    psychological chaos — but in a good way. You're not harming anyone,
    but you are benevolently torturing them into buying so they've got to
    have what you're selling.
