- **How to get better every day** — Publicity master Paul Hartunian has an
interesting way of learning bullets. Whenever he's writing anything down,
whether it be a phone message or writing instructions, or writing notes to
himself... he puts everything in bullet form. It's a great habit to get in to
and will help you get better at not just bullet writing, but all copywriting
every time you do it.

- **To be the best, study the best** — I mentioned some names throughout
this book already like David Deutsch, Gary Bencivenga, Gary Halbert,
Mel Martin, John Carlton, Scott Haines, Parris Lampropoulos, and Bob
Bly. All these guys are incredible bullet writers. As is Clayton Makepeace,
Carline Anglade-Cole, Kim Schwalm, and any A-list copywriter who
writes lots of bullets. Study them all, anything you can get your hands
on, and especially their bullets. You can also get great at this by studying
high selling magazine covers at the news stand. Like Cosmo, Mens
Health, and tabloids like the National Enquirer. Look at how these
copywriters — and other great copywriters — approach their bullets.
They're not boring. They're not plain vanilla. There's always something
interesting going on in them, and they keep people off balance and that's
the key. Even better: Write their bullets out by hand. Every day. As often
as possible, until writing great bullets is second nature to you.
