**Silence Even The Most Stubborn**
**“Inner Skeptic” In Your Prospect’s Brain!**

Doing this (especially in your bullets — which are emotional by their very
nature) is also going to make your ads stick out from all the hyped-up ads your
competition runs. Sometimes bullets can cross some lines where they just
sound like complete bull crap, even though it’s also completely legit. But you
can “blunt” that effect by sticking in something to prove what you’re saying is
credible and stifling the prospect’s inner skeptic always chanting “that’s bull
crap!” in their mind.

Here’s an example:

-   **A scientifically proven way (discovered by Isaac Newton) to hit
    another man with “sledge hammer” like force. (Regardless of
    your size or speed now.)**

Hitting another man with sledge hammer like force is a stretch for some
people to believe. But, by using verbiage like “a scientifically proven way” and
“discovered by Isaac Newton”, it adds a layer of credibility to the bullet.

-   **The one word Socrates (perhaps the greatest master of
    persuasion who ever lived) used to “sell” people on his ideas —
    even though they were dead set against him before!**

The word “Socrates”, credentialed with “the greatest master of persuasion
who ever lived” gives a double-whammy of credibility to an otherwise
potentially hyped-up claim. If I’d just said “here’s how you can sell people on
your ideas even if they’re dead set against you!” it wouldn’t have the depth it
does.
