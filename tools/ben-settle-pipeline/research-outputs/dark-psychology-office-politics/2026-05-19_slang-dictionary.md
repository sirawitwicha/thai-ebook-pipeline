# Slang & Vernacular Dictionary: Dark Psychology of Office Politics
**120+ entries — the market's exact language**

## Power & Manipulation Tactics (How they describe what's done to them)

1. **gaslighting** — "making someone think that not only what you said never happened but that you are losing it" — systematically destroying someone's grip on reality. Mentioned in nearly every comment thread.

2. **grey rock / gray rock** — "Ignore, ignore, ignore. Stay super positive, and never break from that character." Becoming so boring and unreactive that manipulators lose interest and move to easier targets. "Grey rock is the best — they are desperately looking for a reaction, desperate for attention and incredibly insecure."

3. **flying monkeys** — Accomplices a narcissistic boss deploys to do their dirty work. From Wizard of Oz, popularized in narcissistic abuse literature. "the highest upper hand and minions who support them."

4. **quiet firing** — "The Dark Psychology Behind Getting Pushed Out Without Warning." Making a job so miserable the employee resigns "voluntarily" so the company avoids severance/legal exposure.

5. **quiet quitting** — Doing the bare minimum required, emotionally disengaging, refusing to go "above and beyond." The employee's defensive response to exploitation.

6. **credit theft** — "My boss will disagree with me or tell me Im wrong in front of everyone and then 5 minutes later will say exactly what I was saying like its his idea." The #1 daily frustration across all data.

7. **managing up** — Managing your boss's perception of you rather than the work itself. The game within the game. "spend more energy strategizing how to navigate my own office than I do on the actual work."

8. **throwing under the bus** — Scapegoating someone to protect yourself or your boss. "They find a scapegoat to blame. Upper management never faces the consequences."

9. **brown-nosing / kissing ass** — Sycophantic behavior toward superiors. "Disgusting how people kiss ass and let 'superiors' walk on them."

10. **playing the game** — Engaging in office politics strategically. "I'm not a believer that great workers or bosses are losers... What I'm suggesting is being true to self whether at work or after work."

11. **the pecking order** — Informal workplace hierarchy. "Working at a dog grooming salon taught me a lot about power hierarchy. When the alpha is anxious, the entire pack is anxious."

12. **moving the goalposts** — Constantly changing expectations so the target can never succeed. Implied in "Nothing the victim does is ever satisfactory" and "Never Appreciating" tactic.

13. **baiting** — "Provoking a reaction through sensational jabs, then using the target's natural response to claim victimhood." The most insidious form: they provoke you, you react, they play victim.

14. **love bombing** — Excessive flattery used as setup for exploitation. "Building Your Confidence" — "Excessive flattery used to hook someone, then exploit them."

15. **projection** — "The manipulator's own shortcomings are reframed as the victim's faults." — "If you had given me a better project to handle, I would have done a better job."

16. **triangulation** — Bringing a third party into a conflict to validate the manipulator's position and isolate the target. Implied in how gossip networks function.

17. **dog-eat-dog world** — Characterization of workplace as predatory. "It's a dog-eat-dog world out there."

18. **playing the victim card** — When confronted, manipulators "play the victim card" — flipping the narrative so the actual target looks like the aggressor.

19. **feigning innocence** — "Feign innocence and pretend to be confused" when confronted about manipulation.

20. **moving in silence** — "Playing dumb while observing everything... that's the tactic nobody teaches you, but everyone needs." Strategic non-disclosure.

21. **Machiavellian** — The master frame for this entire niche. "Machiavelli didn't teach cruelty, he taught control." Used as both warning and aspiration.

22. **narcissist / narcissistic** — The most common label for toxic bosses. Used interchangeably with "manipulator," "toxic person," "sociopath."

23. **pathological workplace** — Jordan Peterson's term. A fundamentally toxic organization where dysfunction is baked into the structure, not just individual bad actors.

24. **lateral violence** — Peer-to-peer workplace aggression. "In the nursing field, confronting tyranny comes with a high risk of disownership from the group... Lateral violence is a solid thing in nursing."

25. **the system is rigged** — "The system is rigged against honest people." Core belief of this market.

26. **political animal** — Someone who naturally thrives on workplace politics. Used with a mix of disdain and grudging respect.

27. **the game** — Shorthand for the entire political ecosystem. "You've been playing a game you don't know the rules to." "I accept my losing position in the corporate world."

28. **minions** — The enablers and flying monkeys who support toxic leadership. "the highest upper hand and minions who support them."

29. **target on your back** — Being marked for elimination. "once you have spoken out and defended yourself within this environment, you will be a managerial target."

30. **knives out** — The moment politics turns openly hostile. Implied across multiple comments about coordinated attacks.

## Survival & Counter-Strategies (What they do to fight back)

49. **f**k you money** — "You've got to set yourself up so you've got mobility" — enough savings to quit without another job lined up. The ultimate power move. "The purpose of making money etc is so that you can say no to people."

50. **exit plan** — "Start your exit plan now." The strategic preparation to leave before you're pushed. "keep PLANNING. Keep looking for opportunities to move on."

51. **CYA (Cover Your Ass)** — "I write myself an email... documenting what happened, just in case something weird does happen." Paper trail as self-defense.

52. **paper trail** — Documentation of everything. Emails, notes, records. Your weapon when HR comes calling.

53. **keep your head down** — "I keep my door shut and keep my conversation strictly work-related." Avoidance as strategy.

54. **don't give them ammunition** — "Don't give them no information about yourself… down to what you did over the weekend." Information discipline.

55. **stay in your lane** — Do only your job, nothing more, nothing less. Self-preservation through boundaries.

56. **read the room** — "Able to read the room" — emotional intelligence as both weapon (for manipulators) and shield (for targets).

57. **pick your battles** — "Pick your battles, your poison, don't make any unnecessary enemies, get your words right, & have an exit plan."

58. **stand your ground** — "I'd rather walk out with my backbone and my pride than play a pretentious game of what you can and can't say."

59. **don't take the bait** — "Do not take the bait!" — resisting the provocateur's traps. "They are usually projecting which is lead by emotion with little fact, so call them up on it."

60. **keep your cards close** — "Never reveal all you know." Information as power. Strategic silence.

61. **observe, don't absorb** — Watch the politics without letting them poison you. "Being good in psychology has helped me deal with different kinds of people."

62. **stay unreadable** — "I am unreadable. I am untouchable!" The strategic advantage of opacity. "Every reaction, every over-explanation, every rushed answer is fuel for their playbook."

63. **don't feed the beast** — Starve toxic people of the drama and attention they crave. "ignoring them is their kryptonite, they want the drama and attention, ignoring them leaves them powerless."

64. **kill them with kindness** — Strategic positivity that gives manipulators nothing to work with. "Stay super positive, and never break from that character."

65. **build alliances** — "Not gonna play the office politics... Building strategic relationships, creating alliances — this is some stuff of horrors for an introvert."

66. **play dumb** — "Playing dumb while observing everything.. that's the tactic nobody teaches you, but everyone needs." Feigned ignorance as intelligence gathering.

67. **lawyer up** — "Do your research and get a lawyer who is skilled in fighting the bastards." Legal preparation for the worst case.

68. **document everything** — "Make sure you text and not call. You want it all in writing." The first rule of workplace self-defense.

## Corporate Speak & Euphemisms (What they actually mean)

69. **visibility problem** — "I'm getting feedback that you need more visibility." Translation: you haven't kissed enough ass. Code for "you're not playing politics."

70. **restructuring** — Mass layoff dressed in corporate language. Often used to remove political opponents.

71. **cultural fit** — Whether you play the political game the way management wants. Not about competence.

72. **team player** — Someone who doesn't challenge authority or expose problems. "just a cool way for some to do the work while others lazy around, steal credit."

73. **stakeholder alignment** — Getting the right people to agree before a decision. Often means "pre-wiring meetings so opponents can't object."

74. **managing expectations** — Lowering what you promise so you can overdeliver—or lowering what's expected of you so you can coast.

75. **circle back** — "We'll discuss this never." The corporate version of "no."

76. **let's take this offline** — "Stop talking about this in front of everyone." Damage control.

77. **optics** — How something LOOKS, regardless of reality. The entire game is about optics. "controlling perception matters more than producing results."

78. **bandwidth** — Capacity to take on more work. "I don't have the bandwidth" = "I'm not doing your work for you."

79. **moving forward** — Let's never speak of this failure again. Erases accountability.

80. **alignment** — Getting everyone to agree (or appear to agree). Often achieved through pressure, not genuine consensus.

81. **lean in** — Corporate feminism term (Sheryl Sandberg) now used more broadly for "accept the exploitation willingly."

82. **pivot** — Career change, often forced. "Pivot: The Only Move That Matters is Your Next One."

83. **synergy** — The classic meaningless corporate buzzword. Things working together in ways that somehow justify your job.

84. **deep dive** — Actually looking at something. Made to sound more important than it is.

85. **touch base** — A meeting that could have been an email.

86. **run it up the flagpole** — Propose something to see who salutes (who supports it). Political trial balloon.
